Over the past week, I’ve been planning to launch an online store focused on selling pop-up cards.

About a month ago, I came across a short video that inspired me to turn this creative product into a business venture.

At this point, the idea is still in its early stages. I’ve begun working on some of the foundational tasks, such as applying for a business license, leasing a domain name, and selecting an independent store service provider. However, these are just the beginning, and there is still much more groundwork to be done.

From the very beginning, I’ve been documenting every decision I make. In just one or two days, I made nearly ten decisions. When I paused to review them, I couldn’t help but wonder—was I too hasty in making these choices?

The video that inspired me featured a series of pop-up cards created from movie footage, which are called "Pop-up cards" abroad. I was truly amazed by it. A few days later, I consulted with a copyright lawyer, who informed me that pop-up cards based on movie material, as well as any derivative works, could not be legally sold. At that point, I nearly gave up on the idea.

However, for some unknown reason, the idea of commercializing pop-up cards started swirling in my mind again. I can’t really explain why—perhaps it was because I came across a post, or maybe I accidentally found an unregistered domain related to Pop-up cards. Whatever the reason, I can honestly say that I wasn’t driven by rational thinking when I reconsidered the idea.

Initially, my plan was to leverage my web development skills to create an independent website service for international trade. However, as I was nearing the completion of the initial development of the authorized server, something made me reconsider my approach. Since my ultimate goal was to run a business, why should I focus on development-related tasks? It clearly seemed unnecessary. So, I began looking for existing, mature solutions. Once I found one, the only remaining issue was money. If I had enough funds to purchase these solutions, I might have already moved to the next stage. But, obviously, I didn’t.

The lack of startup capital forced me to pause and reevaluate every decision I had made. More importantly, during this time, I had the opportunity to reposition both myself and my business plan. I realized I needed to rethink some key issues.

In the past, I had never truly run a business, only a couple of unsuccessful attempts. Once, during my internship in Weihai, I wholesaled glow-in-the-dark balloons that could be filled with feathers. After selling just three or four, I stopped after a day. Another time, in college, I tried offering a service to help people air their comforters, but it fizzled out after just a few days. I often acted impulsively, and many of my decisions were based on unrealistic ideas. As for persistence and effort, their value largely depended on whether my decisions were sound.

So, will I fall into the same pattern again? I can answer with certainty: if I continue to follow my intuition without rational scrutiny, I will fail just like before. But without a doubt, I will do everything I can to prevent that from happening. Yes, I must make a change.

In the coming days, I will faithfully document my entrepreneurial journey. Stay tuned!
